Musikladen show (Beat Club)

The 70's introduced to Stevie Wonder on Olympus of musical gods thanks to his strength and inspiration when composing, and the power to the interpretation. Their concerts were multiplying everywhere, and from this season -specifically in the year 1974- is this concert through the German program called Musikladen/Beat Club.
There gives an overview of songs from that first half of the decade accompanied by great musicians; Reggie McBride on bass, Michael Sembello (famous among other things for his song Maniac from Flash Dance) on lead guitar and keyboards, Marlo Henderson on rhythm guitar, Ollie. E. Brown on percussion, and of course, his group of voices female Wonderlove, formed at that time by a very young Deniece Williams, Shirley Brewer, and Lani Groves.

They are thirty minutes of the magic that can only emerge from a whole musical genius playing the following songs:
    1. Jam
    2. Contusion
    3. Higher Ground
    4. Don't You Worry 'Bout A Thing
    5. I Can See The Sun In Late December
    6. He's Misstra Know-It-All
    7. Living For The City
    8. Superstition
