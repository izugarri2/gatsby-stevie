Nelson Mandela;
symbol of freedom

Stevie Wonder, Messenger of Peace of the United Nations, and drive man for equality and human rights, has always expressed his admiration and affection in front of the great man who has devoted much of his life to others, Nelson Mandela. His heart stopped pumping blood, but his existence will remain linked forever as symbol of freedom.

He was prisoner number 466/64, is locked for up 27 years for his peaceful struggle against racial inequality. About him numerous awards around the world for their suffering and struggle to eliminate apartheid, among them the Nobel Peace Prize, and being the first democratically elected president of South Africa. The General Assembly of the United Nations established in 2009 marking the days July 18 on the calendar -Mandela's birthday- as "Mandela Day".
A recognition of many years who dedicated his life helping others, advocate human rights defender and always working for peace. At the first event of celebration, artists such as Aretha Franklin, Alicia Keys, Queen Latifah, and of course, Stevie Wonder were there honoring a one of a kind. The last months have been critical of his poor health, and inevitably the outcome has been his end between us. His punished heart has stopped beating forever.

There have been many years he has lived surrounded by many injustices and suffering, but the love and appreciation of good people will always prevail and keep love alive who dedicated to others.

"Keep Our Love Alive" is the Stevie Wonder's song published in 1990 to sensitize public opinion against the inhuman representing Apartheid in South Africa, and Stevie played in this tribute to a man who fought for ideals that would lead to equality and freedom of all human beings.

Just as when one of those great living beings disappears that has given much of his life dedicating it selflessly to others, Just is thank you knowing that your spirit will always remain with us keep that love alive.
