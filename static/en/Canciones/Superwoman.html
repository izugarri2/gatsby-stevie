Superwoman (Where Were You When I Needed You)


1972 album MUSIC OF MY MIND


Composer * Stevie Wonder
Produced & Arranged * Stevie Wonder
Musicians * Buzzy Feiton: Guitar Solo, Stevie Wonder: all instruments

The 70's were beginning and Stevie Wonder was in the process of separating with Syreeta Wright, with whom he had been married for 18 months. From there came one of the most beautiful heartbreak songs ever composed. It belongs to his album Music of my Mind, and it was his first single. We are talking about of "Superwoman (Where Were You When I Needed You), a serene and intropective song that talks about his relationship with Syreeta. It is divided into two very different parts, creating its own identity outside of the Motown sound.

The first -Superwoman- starts off with a synth sound that gives a feeling of ambient buoyancy. Talking about "Mary" -which is really Rita (Syreeta's birth name)- who wants to leave her old life to become a movie star. In the lyrics we can hear "Mary wants to be a superwoman and try to boss the bull around". With the bull he refers to himself, since "Bull" is how Stevie identifies himself by his zodiac sign -Taurus- and as he later named his production company. It's the rejection oto a dominant, or very independent woman, and in turn an attempt to reach an understanding with her.

The second part -Where Were You When I Needed You- starts with a transition where the sensation of buoyancy immerses you in an ethereal space, meeting us to a Stevie pain filled over and over again wondering over and over again "Where Where You When I Needed You?", and why hasn't she returned as soon as he expected, using the seasons as metaphors. This part could be considered a reworking of his previous work on Where I'm Coming From with the song "Never Dreamed You'd Leave in Summer".

A song full of feelings where Stevie plays practically all the instruments, and as always, interpreted in a masterful way. A song that talks about the relationship with a woman who marked him for life -Syreeta- and that despite his separation, he emotionally continued with a deep friendship until his death after a period of fighting against cancer in 2004.
