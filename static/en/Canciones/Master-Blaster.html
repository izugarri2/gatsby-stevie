Master Blaster (jammin')


1980 album HOTTER THAN JULY


Composer * Stevie Wonder
Produced & Arranged * Stevie Wonder
Musicians * Nathan Watts : Bass, Ben Bridges: Guitars, Dennis Davis: Drums, Earl DeRoen: Percussion, Isaiah Sanders: Organo, Hank Redd: Saxophone, Larry Gittens: trumpet, M.Holcolm, A.Winbush, S.Brewer, & A.Evans: Background Vocals, Stevie Wonder: all instruments

Video

The song, with rhythm and reggae style, is an ode to reggae legend Bob Marley. Wonder had several performances with Marley on tour in the US in the autumn of 1980. The letter mentions "the children of Jah" and the end of civil war in Zimbabwe.

It was a great success, spending seven weeks at number one on the Billboard R&B singles chart, reaching number five on Billboard's pop singles chart in the fall of 1980[1] and peaking at number two on the UK Singles chart. The song includes the lyrics "hotter than july" album's title.
